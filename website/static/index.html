🌍 Air Quality Double Jeopardy Dashboard

Identifying Communities Facing Chronic AND Acute Pollution Burden (2021-2024)

Project Summary: This dashboard analyzes EPA Air Quality Index data from 2021-2024 to identify U.S. counties experiencing Double Jeopardy—communities suffering from both persistently poor daily air quality AND dangerous pollution spikes.

Double Jeopardy = High Chronic + High Acute

A county qualifies as Double Jeopardy if it meets BOTH criteria:

  • High Chronic Exposure: 4-year average Median AQI ≥ 90th percentile
    → Persistent daily pollution burden
  • High Acute Exposure: 4-year average Max AQI ≥ 90th percentile
    → Dangerous pollution spikes

Why it matters: These communities face a compounding health burden— their residents never get relief from poor air quality AND face periodic dangerous episodes.

📈 Severity Score Analysis

Combined Pollution Burden Metric

What is the Severity Score? A single metric that combines both chronic and acute pollution exposure into one comparable number. It normalizes both dimensions to a 0-1 scale and averages them.

Normalization Formula

Normalized = (Value - Min) / (Max - Min)

Scales all values between 0 (best) and 1 (worst).

Severity Score Formula

Severity = (Norm_Chronic + Norm_Acute) / 2

Equal weighting of both dimensions.

📐 Methodology

Data Processing Pipeline

  • Data Source: EPA Air Quality Index Annual Summary files (2021-2024)
  • Geographic Unit: U.S. Counties
  • Aggregation: 4-year average of annual Median AQI and Max AQI per county
  • Threshold Calculation: 90th percentile computed from the full dataset

Key Metrics Defined

  • Mean Median AQI: Average of yearly Median AQI values. Represents typical daily air quality.
  • Mean Max AQI: Average of yearly Max AQI values. Represents peak pollution events.
  • Severity Score: (Norm Median + Norm Max) / 2. A single 0-1 metric for ranking.

Risk Categories

  • Low Risk Below 90th %ile on both metrics
  • High Chronic Above 90th %ile for Median AQI only
  • High Acute Above 90th %ile for Max AQI only
  • Double Jeopardy Above 90th %ile for BOTH
Important Limitations:
  • Not all counties have continuous air quality monitoring.
  • Extreme Max AQI from wildfires can skew averages.
  • 4-year averages smooth out year-to-year variation.
  • Analysis treats all counties equally (no population weighting).
  • AQI is a composite index — counties may face different primary pollutants.
